Finding true value in college

It's not difficult to find the cost of tuition at any given college. What's not as easy to find is the actual cost a college's students are paying, how much debt they are accumulating, and how much they earn after graduating. All of these factors contribute to the true value of a college and are factors in choosing a school to attend.

About the data

Source: Funding data comes from the State Higher Education Executive Officers Association; all other data from the U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard.

Data Descriptions: State Funding is the state's higher education budget divided by the number of enrolled students; historical funding data is adjusted for inflation. Median Debt for each school is the total median debt for students who graduate; Median Earnings for each school gives the median of graduates' salaries ten years after graduation; and the state values give the median of the individal schools' debt and earnings. Net Cost is the average price students pay to attend after federal, state, and school aid packages, including tuition and fees, textbooks, and living expenses. Graduation Rate gives the percentage of first-time, full-time first year students who complete a bachelor's degree within six years. Repayment Rate is the percent of students who are actively paying down their student loans three years after graduation.
